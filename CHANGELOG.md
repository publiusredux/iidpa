# Changelog — Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA)

All substantive changes to the IIDPA draft are documented here by version. Changes are organized by category rather than section number to facilitate review by legal and policy audiences.

---

## v2.0 — April 2026

Major structural revision. The bill was subjected to multi-round legal, constitutional, and implementation review. Changes fall into five categories: legal hardening, structural additions, governance architecture, drafting refinements, and documentation.

---

### Legal Hardening

**Enforcement standard clarification**
- Section 1.4 and Section 2.1 revised to require "reasonable articulable suspicion of a violation of federal immigration law or other criminal offense" — replacing prior language that referenced criminal offenses only, resolving a civil/criminal threshold mismatch in the original enforcement framework
- Section 2.1 explicitly scoped to interior enforcement, with border checkpoint and border zone authority preserved under existing 8 U.S.C. §1357(a)(3) authority

**Evidentiary exclusion**
- Section 2.2 revised to clarify that prohibited factors are not individually sufficient for reasonable suspicion but may be considered in a totality-of-circumstances analysis consistent with constitutional precedent
- Section 2.3 cross-referenced to Section 2.5 pretextual stop findings

**Pretextual stop accountability**
- Section 2.5 added: agency-level civil monetary penalties for substantiated patterns of pretextual stops; suspension of federal grant funding under 34 U.S.C. §10151 et seq. for systemic violations; evidence inadmissibility for pattern findings regardless of individual stop threshold; individual officer good-faith carve-out preserved

**Asylum and non-refoulement**
- Section 3A added as standalone section: explicit preservation of INA §208 asylum eligibility, INA §241(b)(3) withholding of removal, Convention Against Torture protections, and non-refoulement obligations under the 1967 Refugee Protocol
- Strike tolling during pending asylum claims established with reinstatement upon denial
- Door 1 explicitly unavailable to individuals with pending or viable asylum claims
- Section 5.3 added cross-referencing asylum tolling and minor exemption

**Door 1 asylum waiver**
- Section 4.4 revised: Door 1 election constitutes waiver of defensive asylum claims based on pre-departure circumstances; post-departure claims based on materially new evidence preserved; claims filed at port of entry following Door 1 departure presumptively barred absent clear and convincing evidence of new post-departure persecution; waiver administered in applicant's primary language with access to counsel

**Strike system due process**
- Section 5.2 revised to require mandatory individualized hearing before permanent Strike 2 bar applies

**Zadvydas compliance**
- Section 4.1.4 added: supervised release with reporting requirements for stateless persons where removal is not reasonably foreseeable, consistent with *Zadvydas v. Davis*, 533 U.S. 678 (2001)

### Structural Additions

**DACA and deferred action transition**
- Section 4.0 added: existing deferred action recipients grandfathered into Door 2 eligibility without restarting continuous residence clock; DACA-eligible individuals who never applied treated as standard Door 2 applicants; disqualifying offenses remain applicable

**Stateless persons**
- Section 4.1 added: routing of stateless persons to Doors 2-4; USCIS statelessness determination process; manufactured statelessness exclusion; persecution-based denationalization carve-out for state-imposed denationalization resulting from ethnic persecution, religious persecution, political retaliation, or crimes against humanity; *Zadvydas* compliance

**Unaccompanied minors**
- Section 4.2 added: TVPRA and *Flores* settlement deference; Strike exemption for minors; 180-day aging-out transition with prior unlawful presence not counted against applicant; serious juvenile offense carve-out for homicide, rape or sexual assault, aggravated assault causing serious bodily injury, and terrorism-related conduct; mandatory individualized hearing consistent with *Miller v. Alabama* and *Roper v. Simmons*; rehabilitation exception

**Labor Bridge**
- Section 4.13 added: Strike 1 recipients employed at E-Verify compliant firms eligible for 90-day conversion to Door 3 WCV status; 5-year Door 2 bar preserved; conversion counts as standard WCV renewal

**Sectoral Staggering Authority**
- Section 5.4 added: joint USDA/DOL certification required at 15% projected labor shortfall threshold using JOLTS and FLS data; 180-day increments with re-certification requirement; 24-month hard cap with no exceptions; Wind-Down WCV issued to affected workers; 60-day departure window upon expiration with automatic Strike 2 for non-compliance; Door 1 Priority Queue available to Wind-Down WCV holders who depart within window on equal terms with standard Door 1 electors

**WCV cliff resolution**
- Section 4.12 revised: mandatory year-4 transition assessment; third renewal option contingent on demonstrated Door 2 progress; supervised departure option under Door 1 terms for holders unable to qualify for Door 2; Strike 1 adjudication pathway preserving WCV status pending resolution

**Administrative off-ramp**
- IIDPA Adjudication Office established within USCIS for non-adversarial processing of uncontested Door 2 and Door 3 applications, reserving EOIR jurisdiction for contested denials, Strike challenges, and formal appeals

**Door 1 Priority Queue**
- Section 4.5 revised: Door 1 electors placed in priority queue for H-2A and WCV nonimmigrant visa categories; Wind-Down WCV holders departing within 60-day window receive equal priority queue access

**Bilateral Return Protocols**
- Section 9 revised: Secretary of State directed to negotiate Expedited Return Protocols with high-volume sending nations within 18 months; cooperating nations eligible for reintegration assistance from penalty fee revenues

### Governance Architecture

**Definitions Governance — Section 14 added**
- Tri-layer interpretive authority: DHS (operational), USCIS (benefits eligibility), DOJ/EOIR (adjudicatory)
- Conflict resolution hierarchy: DHS governs unless dispute relates solely to adjudicatory procedure
- Skidmore-weight deference for agency interpretations issued through notice-and-comment rulemaking
- Notice-and-comment requirement for all definitional modifications; informal guidance and policy memoranda prohibited from modifying statutory definitions
- Consistency requirement across DHS, USCIS, DOL, and EOIR
- Section 14.5: INA §242 judicial review standards preserved; where direct conflict arises between agency interpretive authority and INA §242 framework, standards and procedures of INA §242 control

**Implementation Authority — Section 15 added**
- Emergency hiring authority under 5 U.S.C. §§2103 and 3302: expedited hiring for immigration judges, asylum officers, USCIS adjudicators, and IT security personnel; term-limited adjudication appointments; reemployed annuitants; OPM priority hiring track within 90 days
- IT procurement authority: streamlined acquisition up to $150 million per contract action; interagency contracting authority; national security system designation; NIST SP 800-53 cybersecurity governance framework
- Fund transfer and reprogramming authority: 15% cap per appropriation account per fiscal year; 30-day advance notification to Appropriations Committees for actions exceeding $10 million; authority expires at 7-year mark
- IIDPA Program Management Office: established within DHS within 90 days; administrative and operational authority only; no adjudicatory authority; interoperability certification authority; quarterly implementation reports to Congress

**Federal Preemption — Section 16 added**
- Express field preemption of civil immigration enforcement procedures, compliance pathways, and immigration status determination systems
- Conditional preemption of state employer verification systems tied to E-Verify phase-in date; state immigration status databases preempted upon DHS certification of integrated system operational status
- Savings clause: state criminal law enforcement, cooperation consistent with DHS protocols, general applicability state laws, and consistent state employer conduct laws preserved
- Data system exclusivity: unauthorized state data system integration constitutes basis for suspension of federal law enforcement grant funding

### Drafting Refinements

**Enforcement standard language**
- "Reasonable suspicion of a specific criminal offense" replaced with "reasonable articulable suspicion of a violation of federal immigration law or other criminal offense" throughout Sections 1.4 and 2.1

**Funding floor anti-reclassification**
- Section 9 baseline appropriation revised: explicit prohibition on offset, rescission, or reprogramming based on fluctuations in penalty or fee revenue; mandatory funding for operational continuity designation

**Court capacity benchmarking**
- Section 6 revised: capacity benchmarks include minimum staffing levels, per-judge caseload targets, and processing time standards; mandatory supplemental appropriation trigger of up to $500 million upon missed benchmarks; corrective action plan discharge mechanism with 60-day committee discharge eligibility

**Appeals forum specification**
- Section 8.2 revised: EOIR in first instance; BIA on further appeal; appropriate U.S. Circuit Court of Appeals consistent with INA §242; de novo review of legal determinations; substantial evidence standard for factual findings; 90-day decision mandate with automatic BIA review upon failure

**Anti-fragmentation**
- Section 4.10 revised: 26 U.S.C. §414(b)-(c) aggregation for 50-employee threshold; 24-month lookback with rebuttable presumption of evasion; Good Faith Compliance Certificate via DOL-approved third-party audit within first 12 months; post-audit fragmentation receives no safe harbor; audit costs tax-deductible under 26 U.S.C. §162

**Definition additions — Section 1.5**
- Aging-out, Wind-Down WCV, Existing deferred action recipient, Manufactured statelessness, Stateless person, Unaccompanied minor, Approved third-party auditor, Common control, Good Faith Compliance Certificate, Sectoral staggering added or revised
