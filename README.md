# Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA)

A proposed federal legislative framework for comprehensive immigration reform.

**Status:** Draft legislation (not introduced in Congress)

> This repository is intended for legal-technical review, not political advocacy.

---

## Scope and What This Is

The IIDPA is a full draft statute — not a policy paper, not a white paper, not a position statement. It is drafted in the style of federal legislation, with statutory citations, defined terms, enforcement mechanisms, fiscal provisions, and implementing authority.

This document is intended for analysis of statutory structure, administrative feasibility, and legal coherence. It does not represent enacted law and has no legal force or effect.

It is published here as an open draft for public review, critique, and improvement.

---

## The Problem This Draft Addresses

Current U.S. immigration policy debates tend to converge on two broad approaches:

- Universal removal of individuals without lawful status
- Universal regularization or amnesty for individuals without lawful status

Each approach faces constraints in statutory authorization, administrative capacity, and enforceability under current federal immigration law.

This draft proposes a structured statutory framework intended to align enforcement, due process, labor market realities, and administrative feasibility within existing constitutional and immigration law constraints.

---

## Core Architecture: The Four Doors

The bill organizes compliance options into four distinct pathways:

| Door | Name | Structure | Summary |
|------|------|-----------|---------|
| **Door 1** | Enhanced Voluntary Departure | Voluntary exit / penalty waiver | Voluntary departure with no removal order, no Strike accrual, and priority queue eligibility for future lawful return |
| **Door 2** | Residency Track | Long-term conditional residency | Conditional residency for long-term residents meeting continuous presence, tax compliance, and conduct requirements, with LPR eligibility upon completion |
| **Door 3** | Labor Integrity Track | Employment-linked legal status transition | Worker Compliance Visa for employed individuals at E-Verify compliant firms, with structured transition pathway to Door 2 |
| **Door 4** | National Service Track | Service-based conditional residency | Conditional residency through 4 years of military or civilian national service, with fast-track naturalization eligibility |

The system is structured so that lawful compliance pathways are more operationally and legally advantageous than continued unlawful presence. Unlawful presence after enactment carries escalating consequences through the Two-Strike system.

---

## Two-Strike System

| Strike | Trigger | Consequences |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Strike 1** | First post-enactment unlawful entry or overstay | 5-year bar from Doors 2-4; eligible for Door 1 or INA §240 proceedings |
| **Strike 2** | Second post-enactment violation | Permanent ineligibility for all Doors; permanent re-entry bar |

Full due process protections apply at every stage. No removal order issues without INA §240 adjudication.

## Key Design Features

**Constitutional Guardrails**
Enforcement standards are anchored to Fourth and Fifth Amendment protections throughout. Stops require reasonable articulable suspicion of a federal immigration law violation or criminal offense. Race, ethnicity, national origin, language, accent, and appearance alone are prohibited bases for enforcement action.

**Labor Market Protection**
The Sectoral Staggering Authority (Section 5.4) is designed to prevent enforcement from triggering acute supply chain disruption. It is triggered only by joint USDA/DOL certification of a projected 15% labor shortfall in a specific NAICS sector and allows phased departures over a capped 24-month period.

**Employer Integrity**
E-Verify phase-in over 5 years with anti-fragmentation provisions under 26 U.S.C. §414(b)-(c) preventing large employers from splitting into shell companies to evade compliance. Safe Harbor audits available for good-faith employers in the first 12 months.

**Asylum and Humanitarian Protections**
Nothing in the bill modifies non-refoulement obligations, asylum eligibility under INA §208, or withholding of removal under INA §241(b)(3). Strikes are tolled during pending asylum claims.

**Governance Architecture**
Sections 14-16 establish the administrative operating system for the bill: interpretive authority hierarchy, implementation machinery, and federal preemption of conflicting state enforcement frameworks.

## Known Limitations

No legislative draft of this scope is complete. Areas requiring further development before any enactment consideration include:

- Pre-enactment consultation with DOJ, DoD, DHS, DOL, OMB, and relevant Congressional committees to identify statutory conflicts
- CBO scoring (required within 120 days of enactment under Section 9)
- Regulatory development by DHS and USCIS within the 180-day implementation window
- Litigation risk assessment under APA, INA §242, and constitutional challenges (forthcoming in `/docs`)
- Failure mode analysis across litigation, implementation, and regulatory drift scenarios (forthcoming in `/docs`)
