## Methodology Overview

This simulated scoring relies on CBO-style conventions and publicly available sources:

- DHS Estimated Unauthorized Resident Population (EURP), 2023 edition
- USCIS adjudication and processing cost models (FY2018-FY2023)
- Social Security Administration payroll-compliance elasticity models
- Border technology procurement data via CBP Acquisition Life Cycle documentation
- E-Verify cost estimates referencing DHS's "Verification Modernization Life Cycle Cost Estimate" (FY2016)
- Historical CBO scores for comparable bills (e.g., S.744 (2013), Dignity Act (2023))

**Data Vintage:**
Where possible, FY2023 datasets were used; fiscal projections are adjusted to 2025 dollars.

**v2.0 Scope Note:**
This revision updates the fiscal analysis to reflect substantive additions in v2.0, including the mandatory baseline appropriation floor (Section 9), IIDPA Program Management Office (Section 15), EOIR court expansion appropriation (Section 9), IIDPA Adjudication Office within USCIS (Section 9), Door 1 surge capacity funding (Section 9), Sectoral Staggering Authority (Section 5.4), and Bilateral Return Protocol negotiation costs (Section 9).

**Disclaimer:**
This is a preliminary simulation. Actual CBO scoring would vary depending on final legislative text, participation rates, agency rulemaking, and economic assumptions.

## I. Overview

This document provides an illustrative approximation of how the Congressional Budget Office (CBO) might evaluate the fiscal impact of the Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA) over a standard 10-year budget window (FY2026-2035). The analysis follows CBO formatting conventions, using conservative assumptions drawn from public DHS data, historical CBO reports, and federal cost models.

IIDPA is projected to have a **neutral to modestly deficit-reducing** impact over 10 years, with most costs occurring early and most revenue emerging in mid- to late-window. The v2.0 governance architecture additions increase near-term implementation costs but strengthen long-term operational resilience and revenue capture.

---

## II. Key Baseline Assumptions

- **Participation Rate:** 30-50% uptake for Doors 1-4
- **Population Scope:** Several million unauthorized individuals reside in the U.S.
- **Budget Window:** FY2026-2035
- **Revenue Sources:** Application fees, visa fees, employer penalties, increased tax compliance
- **Savings Drivers:** Reduced unlawful presence, fewer enforcement disparities, reduced backlogs, administrative off-ramp reducing EOIR caseload costs
- **Economic Effects:** Legal workforce expansion increases GDP and tax receipts
- **v2.0 Additions:** Baseline appropriation floor treated as mandatory spending; PMO, Adjudication Office, and court expansion costs added to implementation outlays

Likely **neutral to modestly positive** under mid-range participation assumptions, given capped discretionary spending, mandatory baseline floor, and strong fee and penalty offsets. High-end cost scenarios with low participation could produce modest deficit impact in early years.

### Dynamic Scoring:
If GDP effects materialize at midpoint projections:
- Additional **$10B-$20B** in federal revenue is plausible
- Legal workforce participation and E-Verify compliance strengthen the long-term outlook

## VI. Sensitivity & Uncertainty Analysis

| Variable | Low Scenario | High Scenario |
|----------|-------------|---------------|
| Participation rate | 20% — lower revenue, lower processing cost | 60% — higher revenue, higher processing cost |
| E-Verify compliance | Strong — more penalty revenue | Weak — lower revenue, higher audit cost |
| IT procurement | On schedule — costs within window | Delayed — costs shift to later years |
| Sectoral Staggering use | Minimal — lower Wind-Down WCV costs | Frequent — higher temporary status administration costs |
| Economic conditions | Downturn — reduces payroll revenue | Growth — accelerates revenue capture |

CBO would refine assumptions with internal DHS, SSA, and DOL operational datasets and microsimulation modeling.

## IX. Conclusion

Under standard CBO-style scoring conventions, IIDPA v2.0 is projected to:

- Be **deficit-neutral to modestly deficit-reducing** under mid-range participation assumptions over 10 years
- Carry higher near-term implementation costs than v1.0, offset by stronger governance and revenue architecture
- Modernize immigration enforcement with operational resilience built into statute
- Expand legal workforce participation and payroll compliance
- Reduce long-term unlawful presence with a structured fiscal foundation
- Avoid uncapped mandatory obligations outside the baseline appropriation floor

This assessment is illustrative and **not** an official CBO estimate. A formal CBO score is required within 120 days of enactment per Section 9.
