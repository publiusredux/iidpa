# Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA)
### One-Page Summary
Prepared by **Publius Redux**

---

## Purpose

The Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA) establishes a balanced, constitutional framework to reduce long-term unlawful presence, strengthen labor enforcement, protect civil liberties, and create lawful, orderly compliance pathways. It proposes a structured statutory alternative to both mass amnesty and mass deportation by combining enforcement, due process, and legal pathways within existing constitutional and immigration law constraints.

---

## Key Goals

- Restore lawful immigration processes
- Reduce exploitation and unlawful hiring
- Protect constitutional rights during enforcement
- Provide clear, structured compliance options
- Strengthen border security and national security
- Modernize labor and service-based immigration mechanisms

---

## Core Components

### 1. Enforcement Limits & Civil Liberties (Section 2)

- No detentions or ID demands without reasonable articulable suspicion of a federal immigration law violation or criminal offense — scoped to interior enforcement
- Prohibits enforcement based solely on race, ethnicity, language, accent, or perceived status; totality-of-circumstances analysis preserved
- Agency-level civil monetary penalties for substantiated patterns of pretextual stops; federal grant funding suspension for systemic violations
- Evidence from egregious constitutional violations inadmissible in civil proceedings
- Mandatory DHS data-minimization standards

### 2. Disqualifying Offenses (Section 3)

Ineligible for all pathways:

- Aggravated felonies (INA §101(a)(43))
- Crimes of violence (18 U.S.C. §16)
- Felony crimes against children
- Sex-offender registration violations
- Drug trafficking (21 U.S.C. §§841, 846)
- Terrorism-related offenses (INA §212(a)(3))

### 3. Asylum & Humanitarian Protections (Section 3A)

- Asylum eligibility, withholding of removal, and non-refoulement obligations fully preserved
- Strikes tolled during pending asylum claims
- Door 1 unavailable to individuals with pending or viable asylum claims

---

## Four Compliance Pathways ("Four Doors")

| Door | Name | Key Requirements | Outcome |
|------|------|-----------------|---------|
| **1** | Enhanced Voluntary Departure | Biometric enrollment; one-time use; knowing waiver of INA §240 | No removal order; no Strike; Priority Queue for lawful return |
| **2** | Residency Track | 4-8 years continuous presence; tax compliance; English proficiency | Conditional Residency → LPR → citizenship eligibility |
| **3** | Labor Integrity Track | E-Verify compliant employer; identity verification; tax compliance | Worker Compliance Visa (WCV) with pathway to Door 2 |
| **4** | National Service Track | 4 years military or civilian service | Conditional Residency; LPR; fast-track naturalization |

**Transition provisions** address existing DACA recipients, stateless persons, and unaccompanied minors before the Four Doors framework applies.

---

## Two-Strike System (Section 5)

| Strike | Trigger | Consequences |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Strike 1** | First post-enactment unlawful entry or overstay | 5-year bar from Doors 2-4; Door 1 or INA §240 proceedings; Labor Bridge available for qualifying employed individuals |
| **Strike 2** | Second post-enactment violation | Permanent ineligibility for all Doors; permanent re-entry bar; mandatory individualized hearing; narrow humanitarian waiver |

**Sectoral Staggering:** Where joint USDA/DOL data confirms a 15% projected labor shortfall in a specific sector, DHS may authorize phased departures over a capped 24-month period to prevent acute supply chain disruption.

---

## Border Security & Humanitarian Measures (Section 12)

- $5B for sensors, drones, surveillance, data integration, and Border Patrol hiring and retention
- Family unity and medical hardship waivers for Doors 2-4
- Annual public reporting on encounters, backlogs, and unlawful-presence trends

---

## Fiscal Framework (Section 9)

- Funded through fees, penalties, DHS/DOL reallocations, and capped appropriations of $15-$30B over 10 years
- Mandatory baseline appropriation floor of $1.5B/year for 7 years; non-reclassifiable based on penalty revenue fluctuations
- CBO score required within 120 days of enactment
- No uncapped mandatory spending

---

## Governance Architecture (Sections 14-16)

- **Section 14:** Tri-layer interpretive authority (DHS/USCIS/EOIR) with Skidmore-weight deference and notice-and-comment requirements for definitional changes
- **Section 15:** Emergency hiring authority, streamlined IT procurement, fund transfer authority, and centralized Program Management Office within DHS
- **Section 16:** Express federal preemption of conflicting state immigration enforcement systems and databases; savings clause preserves state criminal enforcement cooperation

---

## Why IIDPA Works

- Protects constitutional rights at every stage while targeting serious offenders for removal
- Reduces unlawful presence sustainably through incentive-aligned pathway design
- Prevents supply chain disruption through data-driven Sectoral Staggering Authority
- Strengthens border and labor enforcement with anti-fragmentation and E-Verify provisions
- Offers realistic compliance options without mass deportation or blanket amnesty
- Establishes governance architecture capable of surviving administration changes and litigation
- Designed for bipartisan consideration
