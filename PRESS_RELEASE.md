# Publius Redux Releases Major Revision of the Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA) — Version 2.0

**WASHINGTON, D.C. —** Independent policy writer *Publius Redux* today released **Version 2.0** of the **Immigration Integrity and Due Process Act (IIDPA)**, a substantially revised full legislative framework designed to restore order to the U.S. immigration system while upholding constitutional rights, strengthening labor enforcement, and providing structured compliance pathways.

The v2.0 revision significantly expands the bill's legal architecture following multi-round review by legal, constitutional, and policy analysts. Key additions include asylum and humanitarian protections, transition provisions for existing deferred action recipients and unaccompanied minors, labor market safety valves, and a full governance architecture covering interpretive authority, implementation machinery, and federal preemption.

> **"The goal from the beginning has been to build something that could actually function as law — not just as a position statement,"** said Publius Redux.
> **"Version 2.0 addresses the gaps that would have made v1.0 vulnerable in court or in implementation. The architecture is now significantly more resilient."**

# A Modern Framework for Lawful, Orderly Immigration

IIDPA addresses failures across border enforcement, labor markets, due-process protections, and administrative capacity. Version 2.0 includes:

## ✔ Four Compliance Pathways ("The Four Doors")

**Door 1 — Enhanced Voluntary Departure**
- One-time, biometric-verified voluntary departure
- No removal order; no Strike applied; penalty waivers
- Knowing and voluntary waiver of INA §240 proceedings
- Priority Queue for H-2A and Worker Compliance Visa categories for future lawful return

**Door 2 — Residency Track (4-8 Years)**
- Continuous residence, tax compliance, English proficiency
- Conditional Residency → LPR → citizenship eligibility
- Uncontested applications processed through non-adversarial USCIS administrative track

**Door 3 — Labor Integrity Track**
- Nationwide E-Verify phase-in over 5 years
- Anti-fragmentation clause under 26 U.S.C. §414(b)-(c) closes shell-company evasion loophole
- Safe Harbor: DOL-approved third-party audit provides Good Faith Compliance Certificate
- Worker Compliance Visa (WCV) with year-4 Door 2 transition assessment
- Labor Bridge: Strike 1 recipients at E-Verify compliant firms eligible for 90-day WCV conversion

**Door 4 — National Service Track**
- Military or civilian service (4 years)
- 2,000 military / 5,000 civilian slots annually
- Fast-track naturalization

---

## ✔ Transition Provisions (New in v2.0)

**Existing Deferred Action Recipients**
Active DACA and deferred action recipients grandfathered into Door 2 without restarting the continuous residence clock.

**Stateless Persons**
Routed to Doors 2-4 where departure cannot be effected; persecution-based denationalization explicitly protected; manufactured statelessness excluded.

**Unaccompanied Minors**
Governed by TVPRA framework; Strikes do not apply; 180-day aging-out transition; serious juvenile offenses addressed through mandatory individualized hearing.

---

## ✔ Asylum and Humanitarian Protections (New in v2.0)

- Asylum eligibility, withholding of removal, and non-refoulement obligations fully preserved
- Strikes tolled during pending asylum claims
- Door 1 unavailable to individuals with pending or viable asylum claims
- Door 1 asylum waiver forecloses defensive claims based on pre-departure circumstances while preserving claims based on new post-departure persecution

---

## ✔ Two-Strike Enforcement System

**Strike 1:**
- First violation after enactment
- 5-year bar from Doors 2-4
- Access to voluntary departure or INA §240 proceedings
- Labor Bridge available for qualifying employed individuals

**Strike 2:**
- Permanent ineligibility for all Doors and re-entry
- Mandatory individualized hearing before permanent bar applies
- Limited humanitarian waivers

**Sectoral Staggering Authority (New in v2.0)**
Where joint USDA/DOL certification confirms a projected 15% labor shortfall in a specific NAICS sector, DHS may authorize phased departures over a capped 24-month period — preventing enforcement from triggering acute supply chain disruption while preserving Strike consequences.

---

## ✔ Constitutional Guardrails

- Reasonable articulable suspicion of a federal immigration law violation or criminal offense required for detention — scoped to interior enforcement
- No profiling based on race, ethnicity, accent, or perceived status; totality-of-circumstances analysis preserved
- Agency-level civil monetary penalties for substantiated pretextual stop patterns; federal grant funding suspension for systemic violations
- Exclusion of evidence from egregious constitutional violations

---

## ✔ Border Security & Labor Enforcement

- $5B for sensors, drones, aerial surveillance, data integration, and Border Patrol hiring and retention
- E-Verify modernization with anti-fragmentation enforcement
- Strong worker protections and fraud reduction
- Secretary of State directed to negotiate Expedited Return Protocols with high-volume sending nations

---

## ✔ Oversight, Appeals, and Accountability

- Immigration Integrity Inspector General with annual reporting on systemic violations, enforcement fairness, Door utilization, and pretextual stop patterns
- Mandatory GAO audits with enforceable corrective deadlines
- 30-day appeals window; EOIR → BIA → Circuit Court appellate structure with defined standards of review
- Up to 10% funding reductions for systemic agency violations

---

## ✔ Governance Architecture (New in v2.0)

**Section 14 — Definitions Governance**
Tri-layer interpretive authority with Skidmore-weight deference and notice-and-comment requirements — prevents administrative drift between administrations.

**Section 15 — Implementation Authority**
Emergency hiring authority, streamlined IT procurement, fund transfer authority, and a centralized Program Management Office within DHS — converts policy design into executable federal program.

**Section 16 — Federal Preemption**
Express preemption of conflicting state immigration enforcement systems and databases; savings clause preserves state criminal enforcement cooperation.
